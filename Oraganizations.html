Marine Environment Protection Authority(MEPA)

Under the requirements of the Marine Pollution Prevention (MPP) Act No. 59 of 1981, the Marine Pollution Prevention Authority (MPPA) was founded in 1991. Until 1994, it was under the control of the Ministry of Ports and Shipping. The Marine Pollution Control Act No. 35 of 2008, which takes into account the growing issues in marine pollution prevention in Sri Lanka as a result of worldwide advancements and increased economic development activities, abolished the Act. The Authority's name was changed to the Marine Environment Protection Authority (MEPA) on January 1, 2009, and it now falls under the Ministry of Urban Development, Coast Conservation, Waste Disposal, and Community Cleanliness jurisdiction. The Authority's law enforcement capacities have been improved as a result of this act, allowing it to deal with any act of marine environment contamination more effectively and efficiently.

Midaya Ceramic Company

From conception to retail shelf, they collaborate with designers, manufacturers, inventors, and artists. They provide a reliable, high-quality platform for designing, sampling, manufacturing, packaging (Gift or Transit), and shipping your creations right to your home. Midaya is a design studio/factory that serves ceramic producers and designers from around the world. For a variety of foreign customers, they produce a wide range of high-quality ceramics.

The Wildlife & Nature Protection Society

The Wildlife and Nature Protection Society of Sri Lanka has a long and illustrious history in Sri Lankan wildlife conservation. The WNPS, which is 126 years old, is the world's third oldest non-governmental organization and is responsible for the establishment of Sri Lanka's Wilpattu and Yala National Parks, as well as the Department of Wildlife Conservation. One of the world's 34 biodiversity hotspots in Sri Lanka. It's also one of the top ten countries most impacted by global warming. Droughts and floods, among other extreme weather occurrences, enhance competition for resources, growing human activities threaten the existence of many species, and rapidly disappearing natural habitats continue to have devastating implications in their natural environment.

Rainforest Protectors of Sri lanka

Rainforest Protectors of Sri Lanka (RPSL) is a non-profit environmental organization whose mission is to protect Sri Lanka's last remaining rainforests and threatened ecosystems, such as the Sinharaja and Kanneliya rainforests, through conservation projects and environmental activism fueled primarily by volunteer support. Through negotiations with relevant authorities and stakeholders, Rainforest Protectors also investigate, report, and identify solutions to serious threats to our rainforests and animals.
